First semester

04.01.02 Activity Diagram

I was introduced to the concept of Activity Diagrams, how they are used to help streamline the process of game design. My extensive knowledge of video games helped me grasp the concept quicker then I expected. After learning about each shape that comes into play concerning the diagram and what they indicate I went to work on my diagram. I made mine in Adobe Illustrator but that was a mistake as I later found out that there are many options online that simplify the process of creating one.

04.02.02 Peer Review

As the title of the assignment is “peer review” it was quite obvious what It would contain. However during this project I learned about the importance of getting feedback from your peers when going through the development faces of game design. In this review I watched five paper prototypes from my peers and answered a pre-made questionnaire as honestly as possible. After getting the reviews from my peers, I went to work on the problems that were pointed out to me. Those things that I worked on were minor details in the prototype that weren’t so clear, so after thinking about how to make those clearer I finished up my prototype.

04.02.03 Asset List and Design Documentation

After being taught how to make an Asset List, I started working with the html code needed for the list. Within the code I documented all my illustrations and UI elements. With all of the code ready I hosted it on my domain. I was also introduced to the concept of Design Documentation and how it’s necessary for the finished product. In my Design Documentation I go into detail about how I work with video game, flat design and kawaii inspirations for my illustrations. Within the document I display my style tile and moodboard. I also show my sketching process for my characters and my UI elements. Finally in the document I also depict a scene from the game.

04.05.02 The Finished Game

In this module I was introduced to JavaScript, because to animate the illustrations I made I would need to do so with JavaScript. The lectures on JavaScript were comprehensible and helped me with the basic understanding of the game. However, during this module I fell quite ill and was in bad spot mentally so I never got to work on the project itself in any presentable way, therefore I have nothing to show except a scene from the game I hoped to make. But looking back now I’m filled with excitement to try on my own. When making this site I did my best to include some animated parts to the website and look forward to learning more.
